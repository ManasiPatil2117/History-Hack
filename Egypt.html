EGYPT

GIZA

Unquestionably a popular tourist destination in Egypt, Giza, or "Al Giza," is home to some of Ancient Egypt's most well-known attractions, most notably the famed Pyramids of Giza. The pyramids of Pharaohs Khufu, Khafra, and Menkaure can be found inside the complex of pyramids. The world's largest pyramid, as well as the largest pyramid in Giza, belonged to Khufu or "Cheop," the second monarch of the Fourth Dynasty.

Abu Simbel

An amazing collection of ancient Egyptian structures, including several rock temples, may be seen in the Egyptian archaeological site of Abu Simbel. The two Temples of Ramesses II are Abu Simbel's most well-known landmarks. One of the most well-known Egyptian pharaohs, Ramesses II, also known as Ramesses the Great or Ramses, was a member of the Nineteenth Dynasty.

Saqqara

The ancient necropolis of Memphis, the ancient Egyptian nation's capital and the city that Menes founded in 3000 BC, is located in Saqqara, Egypt. It is the oldest complete stone structure complex in history and has numerous burial chambers and pyramids. In Saqqara, which is now a UNESCO World Heritage site, there are 11 significant pyramids spread out across 6 miles.
